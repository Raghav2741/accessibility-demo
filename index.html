Text Spacing (WCAG 1.4.12)

Text spacing allows users to adjust line height, word spacing, letter spacing, and paragraph spacing without losing content or functionality.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • People with dyslexia benefit from increased spacing, which improves reading accuracy.
  • Autistic users may find tightly packed text overwhelming or visually stressful.
  • Cognitive fatigue is reduced when text is easier to visually parse.

Timing Adjustable (WCAG 2.2.1)

Users must be able to extend, adjust, or turn off time limits unless timing is essential.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • Users with ADHD may need additional time to complete tasks.
  • Anxiety can increase significantly when users fear sudden timeouts.
  • People with processing or memory challenges need control over pacing.

Pause, Stop, Hide (WCAG 2.2.2)

Moving, blinking, or auto-updating content must have controls to pause, stop, or hide it.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • Autistic users may find motion distracting or distressing.
  • ADHD users may be unable to focus when content constantly moves.
  • Motion can cause sensory overload or migraines.

Three Flashes or Below Threshold (WCAG 2.3.1)

Content must not flash more than three times per second unless below safe thresholds.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • Flashing content can trigger seizures in users with photosensitive epilepsy.
  • It can cause extreme discomfort for autistic users with sensory sensitivity.
  • Fast flashing can induce headaches, dizziness, or anxiety.

Focus Visible (WCAG 2.4.7)

Keyboard focus must always be visible so users know where they are on the page.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • Users who rely on keyboards need clear focus to avoid confusion.
  • Focus visibility reduces cognitive load and navigation errors.
  • It helps users build a predictable mental model of the interface.

Consistent Navigation & Identification (WCAG 3.2.3 & 3.2.4)

Navigation and components must be presented and identified consistently across pages.

Impact on Neurodivergent Users:
  • Inconsistent navigation increases confusion and anxiety.
  • Autistic users rely heavily on predictable patterns.
  • Consistent labels help users with memory or processing challenges.
