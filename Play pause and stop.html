WCAG 2.2.2 – Play, Pause, Stop

This page shows examples of moving content that meets and fails the WCAG requirement to allow users to pause, stop, or hide motion.

✅ Passing Example (Play, Pause, Stop Available)

Why this PASSES:
  • Motion starts automatically
  • User can pause, play, or stop the animation
  • Motion lasts longer than 5 seconds but is controllable
MOVING

❌ Failing Example (No Controls)

Why this FAILS:
  • Animation starts automatically
  • Lasts more than 5 seconds
  • No way for users to pause, stop, or hide motion
NO CONTROL
Accessibility Tip:
Any auto‑moving, scrolling, or blinking content that lasts longer than 5 seconds must provide controls to pause, stop, or hide it — unless the motion is essential.
